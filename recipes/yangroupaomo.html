Yangrou Paomou

Description

Xiâ€™an, being on the silk road, sits at a fascinating middle ground between east and west, only in this case, west means not Europe but the steppes of Central Asia.

This soup reflects that heritage: it blends Chinese spices and flavors (ginger, star anise, sichuan peppercorns) with lamb, a very Central Asian meat, and bread. The bread is almost a homestyle flour tortilla or naan, meant to be ripped apart and doused in the soup, to thicken and dissolve in the broth.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 pounds (3 kg) boneless lamb (mutton, goat or stew-grade beef also work)
  • 10 cups (litres) beef stock
  • 1 tsp (5 ml) fennel seeds
  • 1 tsp (5 ml) Sichuan peppercorns
  • 2 star anise pods
  • 1 small stick cinnamon
  • 2 or 3 dried Thai chiles
  • 2 inches (5 cm) fresh ginger, smashed
  • 5 green onions, trimmed, lightly smashed
  • 1 tsp (5 ml) sea salt, or to taste
  • 2 cups (250 g) flour
  • 2/3 cup water
  • 1/2 tsp baking powder
  • 2 bundles cellophane noodles, soaked in cool water until soft
  • 1/4 cup dried wood ear mushrooms, soaked in water for at least an hour
  • Handful of cilantro, chopped
  • Chili paste or oil, to taste
  • Black vinegar, to taste

Directions

  1. Cut the meat into 1-inch (2 cm) cubes.
  2. Place the meat in a large Dutch oven or soup pot, cover with water, and boil for about 10 minutes to remove the initial fat.
  3. Pour out the water and skim off any scum that forms on top.
  4. Rinse the meat in a colander and rinse out the pot or Dutch oven.
  5. Return the parboiled meat to the pot and add the stock.
  6. If you have a mesh spice ball, use it to hold the fennel seeds and Sichuan peppercorns. If not, use cheesecloth tied with twine.
  7. Add the spice package (or loose spices) to the soup along with star anise, cinnamon, dried chiles, ginger, and green onions.
  8. Bring to a simmer, cover, and cook for 2 hours.
  9. While the soup is simmering, prepare the bread: mix the flour and baking powder together, then add water and knead into a dough.
  10. Cover the dough and let it rise for about 30 minutes.
  11. Heat a wok or frying pan (no oil) to medium-high.
  12. Take small pieces of dough and roll them into circles about 1/3 inch (less than 1 cm) thick.
  13. Slap each dough circle onto the hot pan, browning on each side. Set aside. (Like making tortillas, but thicker.)
  14. Add the soaked noodles and mushrooms to the soup near the end of the cooking time.
  15. Serve the soup with cilantro, vinegar, and chili sauce as condiments.
  16. Break the bread into the stew as a thickener, similar to how fritos are used in chili.
